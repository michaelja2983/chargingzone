Common Electric Charger Connector Types

Type 1

Up to 7kW AC. This socket is typically found on older EVs, such as the original (2010-2017) Nissan Leaf.

Type 2

Up to 6kW AC. The majority of European-made EVs have a Type 2 connector.

CSS

Up to 350kW DC. The most popular form of rapid or ultra-rapid charging connection, CCS car sockets contain a combined alternating current (AC) and DC port.

CHAdeMO

Up to 50kW DC. This connection is favoured by Japanese and Korean car makers. Popular models that use it include the Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV and Nissan Leaf.
